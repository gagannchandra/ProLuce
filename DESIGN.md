# ProLuce Cinematic Design System

## Brand Identity
ProLuce is a premium, highly cinematic catalogue website. The aesthetic evokes the atmosphere of a high-end film studio or an exclusive premiere event. We emphasize drama, immersion, and elegance through deep shadows, subtle lighting effects, and carefully paired typography.

## Color Palette
The color palette is built around absolute darkness, with warm, glowing accents to draw focus.

- **Background (Primary):** `#0a0a0a` (Deep Void Black) - Used for the main body background to create an immersive, theater-like experience.
- **Background (Secondary):** `#141414` (Studio Charcoal) - Used for cards, panels, and distinct sections.
- **Text (Primary):** `#f2f2f2` (Crisp Silver) - High contrast for readability on dark backgrounds.
- **Text (Secondary):** `#a3a3a3` (Muted Ash) - Used for subtitles, metadata, and less prominent information.
- **Accent (Glow/Interactive):** `#d4af37` (Cinematic Gold) - Used sparingly for buttons, hover states, active links, and subtle lighting accents.
- **Border/Divider:** `#262626` (Subtle Grid) - Used for delineating sections without overwhelming the content.

## Typography
We pair an elegant serif for dramatic headlines with a highly legible, modern sans-serif for body copy.

- **Headings (H1, H2, H3):** *Playfair Display* (or similar elegant serif). These should feel like movie titles—dramatic, confident, and beautifully kerned.
- **Body & UI Elements:** *Inter* (or similar clean sans-serif). These must be highly legible, crisp, and neutral to balance the drama of the headings.

## Spacing & Layout
- **The Cinematic Grid:** Layouts should feel expansive. Use wide margins and generous padding to let imagery breathe.
- **Full-Bleed Imagery:** Hero sections and featured content should span the full width of the screen, reminiscent of a movie screen.
- **Negative Space:** Don't be afraid of empty dark areas; they help frame the content.

## Components & Effects
- **Buttons:** Minimalist, often with just a border or a subtle gold accent on hover. Avoid bulky, solid-color buttons unless they are the primary call-to-action.
- **Imagery:** Photography and video should have high contrast, moody lighting, and deep shadows.
- **Hover States:** Interactive elements should feel premium—think slow, smooth transitions (`transition: all 0.4s ease;`), slight scale-ups, or subtle glows (e.g., `box-shadow: 0 0 20px rgba(212, 175, 55, 0.15);`).
- **Glassmorphism:** Use sparingly on top of imagery. A dark, semi-transparent background with a subtle blur can add depth to overlapping elements.
